Xbox: Series X

The Xbox series X is the next-gen console developed by Microsft to bring about the next era of gaming, with incredible 4K support, 8K HDR range, and brand new Xbox Velocity Architecture to provide the fastest and most powerful gaming system ever designed.

The Series X is set to debut in the holiday season of 2020. Like its predecessor, the Xbox One, it will have full back-compatibility with all of its ancestor systems, all the way back to the original 2001 Xbox.

Xbox One

The Xbox One remains a highly powerful system even now, 6 years after its debut. With larger and more taxing games like Halo 5, Sea of Thieves, and Forza Horizon 4 around the corner, the Xbox One was designed to include greater storage and processing power, superior grpahics support, and the elimination of the need to constantly read from a disc throug hthe installation feature.

New titles are still arriving for the Xbox One, even with the coming Series X about to take over as the newest system. Microsoft continues to dedicate to fans who don't intend to purchase the new console right away, making every new game back compatible for the Xbox One and supporting the older system with continued updates.

Xbox 360

The Xbox 360 was the first reimagining of the original Xbox console. With new games like Gears of War, Halo 3, and Call of Duty: Modern Warfare taking the spotlight, the Xbox hardware was improved to support more powerful gameplay, with less laoding times.

360 Titles are available to purhcase and play from the Microsoft store, and are back compatible with the Xbox One and the upcoming series X.

The Original Xbox

The classic Xbox console, created by Microsoft in 2001, was the first system to integrate the four-player splitscreen multiplayer mechanic into its games. Luanching with the Xbox exclusive "Hal: Combat Evolved," Xbox quickly became one of the defining names in the game conosle industry.

Classic Xbox titles are still available now as part of Xbox Game Pass, or even via a hard copy, and can be played on the Xbox 360, One, and the upcoming Series X.
